ACodd.com - School Project

The Project Objective

ACodd.com is a showcase website for stencil artwork done by a Seattle artist. The objective of the site was to keep the design as minimal as possible thus allowing the artwork to shine through. The client wanted the artwork to remain the centerpiece of the site while still providing the user an aesthetically pleasing website and simple navigation. The majority of the clientsâ€™ work consists of portraits of those whom inspire him to pursue his dream as an artist.

Services

Research, Problem Articulation, Storyboarding, Information Architecture, Interaction Design, Wireframe Production, UI Development

Defining the Project

Discovery

ACodd.com is an art gallery and art store. Inspired by Banksy and Shepard Fairey, the client focuses on maintaining a street-feel with his work. The client incorporates influences from Picasso and Pollock with modern day hip-hop and pop-art, to create a unique hybrid of culture and art. The focus demographic is early to late 20s. Originally the clients work was a simple etsy page, which greatly detracted from his work.

Information Architecture

ACodd had a very simple structure. About and shop, the main two category pages shared page titles, but had very different purposes. On one side, I wanted to solely showcase his art and on the other, I wanted to provide pricing information. Doing this would allow his artwork to take center stage without cluttering the page with prices and additional information.

Wireframes

ACodd.com was designed using a 980px 12 column grid system. The design was to remain simple and easy to navigate, allowing the artwork to remain the focal point

Color Palette

To allow the minimalist apprach to the site, I kept the most basic color palette of black and white. This allowed the artwork to stand out much more than it would otherwise.
