BimmerUS - eCommerce School Project

The Project Objective

BimmerUS is a fictional auto parts warehouse for BMW vehicles. This project was meant to provide an overview and detailed look into the way User Interface and User Experience dictates design choices, functionality, features and content. My goal was to design and develop a small to mid-sized e-commerce website from home page to checkout confirmation, while keeping the user’s path in mind. I wanted to create the simplest checkout experience possible, all while still providing necessary information the user may want and need.

Services

Research, Problem Articulation, Storyboarding, Information Architecture, Interaction Design, Wireframe Production, UI Development

Defining the Project

Discovery

BimmerUS was designed to be the go to destination for the do-it-yourselfer looking to find auto parts and accessories to repair or modify their BMW. The average customer would typically be in their late 20’s to 30’s with a background in automotive work. I wanted to provide the customer a favorable experience by allowing them to quickly and easily find the product they needed. Tools such as the car make and model selector was used to assist in this process.

Information Architecture

Being as BimmerUS is an auto parts warehouse, it was inevitable that the navigational structure would be a little cramped. Each part was grouped into a category that made the most sense. Utility buttons were provided in the header to allow the customer to login, view their shopping cart, view their wish list, etc.

Interaction Design

During the interaction design process the goal was to design the most logical process through a shopping cart. The arrows designate the choice a user would make and the page they would be directed to, based off of that choice.

Wireframes

BimmerUS was designed using a 980px 12 column grid system. The focus of the design was to allow the user to fine tune their searches in a simple compact layout. I wanted the design to provide the customer enough information without having to scroll down the page.

Color Palette

The color palette was minimal through the design. Being as the site is content driven, I didn’t want to provide a lot of color to detract from the objective, which was to get the user in and out as quickly as possible.
