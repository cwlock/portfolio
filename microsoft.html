Project 01|Finance Vertical Scenarios

The Project

Over the course of 2 months I produced scenarios within the Financial Service sector that leveraged one or more of the O365 services such as SharePoint, Skype, Exchange or others via Microsoft Graph. These scenarios would then be used to develop sample Add-ins, which we would share with selected partners in order to further advance the Add-in platform.

My Role

My role was to research Financial Advisors, the potential clients who seek their services, and the technologies used in the Financial Services sector, and create thoughtful conceptual designs for the potential industry partners.

Expert Insights and Brainstorming

I collaborated with multiple industry experts to understand the domain. We began by identifying potential scenarios that would help address struggles within the industry and uncover possible solutions for these particular pitfalls.

Strategy

I created sketches, wireframes and low fidelity mockups, along with user personas to communicate the initial vision for the project. This helped answer prior concerns and questions.

Find My Advisor

The Challenge

Help clients of Remote Advisors use a portal enhanced by Outlook services to conveniently self-schedule meetings with their advisor.

The Discovery

We discovered that scheduling preferences were broken down into 3 major age brackets, Gen Y, Gen X and Baby Boomers.

Most preferred one-to-one personal communication methods such as email, formal meetings at the office and phone call, but there was a strong relationship between client satisfaction with their advisors and the number of communication channels provided. The more communication channels that were provided typically led to higher satisfaction.

These insights pointed to a solution that was flexible, allowing users to select dates, times, and the mode of communication all at their fingertips.

The Requirements

Our research highlighted that keeping the application flexible would make the biggest impact.

Subsequently, we focused our efforts on the following set of requirements:

  • Date selection
  • Smart calendar to show only times available for both client and advisor
  • Communication channel type selection
  • Shared document library
"I want to speak with Alice in a form that fits my style and during a time that fits my schedule."

Persona

Justin Enlow has worked as a Real Estate Agent for 5 years. During this time he’s been heavily invested in the stock market. About 3 years ago, he hired Alice Siciliano to help handle his finances due to his limited hours during the day. As of late, he’s become interested in a company who just announced they are going public. Knowing this, he has concluded that he should speak with Alice to see if it’s a good investment option, but since his line of work causes some time constraints, he needs an easy way to see Alice’s availability and schedule a time to meet that fits his schedule, along with Alice’s.

My Process

Whiteboard Sketches

Quick sketches on the whiteboard helped establish the beginning stages of our UI. As these ideas began to take shape we were able to transition them into low-fidelity wireframes.

Wireframes

Once the wireframe process began I tried to morph my sketches into something a bit more understandable. This phase allowed previously unforeseen issues to surface and new ideas to take shape. In the end, we had a set of key UI elements and interactions that would help successfully complete the challenge.

Desktop Mockup

Initially we foresaw this as a desktop graph application, which would allow clients a central location to meet virtually and schedule meetings that worked for both parties. However, our goal was to allow clients to schedule time with advisors anywhere and any time, which transitioned our design onto a mobile interface.

Know Your Client

The Challenge

Help advisors stay current on changes in their clients’ lives and quickly reach out using a set of smart templates in Outlook.

The Discovery

A major challenge we uncovered regarding client advisor relationships is the lack of up-to-date information advisors have on their clients.

As we all know, social media has become a major part of millions of peoples’ lives. The type of information people reveal consists of major and minor life changes that could likely benefit advisors help them cater to these individuals.

The Requirements

Throughout our investigation we discovered that advisors want some means of tracking their clients in order to accurately manage portfolios and make the most educated decisions regarding client finances.

Efforts were focused on the following requirements:

  • Social media connections
  • Smart email templates
  • Smart email updates on client information changes
"I need to stay current in my clients' lives to increase their satisfaction."

Persona

Alice runs her own business as a Certified Financial Planner. As a CFP, she has multiple clients whom she must stay up-to-date with. Currently, she calls, emails, texts, and sets up personal meetings to make sure she is current and that she is accurate in her advice to maintain client satisfaction. A major challenge Alice frequently runs into is failed client communication regarding financially impacting events, such as major purchases, new investments and career changes to name a few.

My Process

Sketches and Sticky Notes

Initially this scenario took quite a different shape. Rather than a whiteboard, I began by sketching out a set of screens and pinning them to a tag board. I then pulled out my weapon of choice, sticky notes. Sketching quick bits of content on the sticky notes and placing them on the board allowed me to rapidly change the design by simply pulling off the old and placing the new in its place.

Wireframes

With the general idea in mind, I began to create very low-fidelity wireframes in order to bring the idea to fruition. Much like the sketching phase, we began to strip the concept to its roots simplifying the design on each pass.

Desktop Mockup

The final product would allow advisors to reach out using smart premade and user made templates. The system would constantly monitor individual clients and search for changes made on the connected social media sources. When changes were detected, email alerts were sent to the advisor and any unread alerts were tracked by the Know Your Client system.

Project 02|Heuristic Analysis

I have omitted and obfuscated some confidential information. The information here is my own and does not necessarily reflect the views of Microsoft.

The Project

A substantial portion of my time was spent on Heuristic Analyses of internal and 3rd party Add-ins. During a single analysis, I would utilize an internal set of Tenets and Traps and based upon my findings I would provide a set of design recommendations. Depending on how closely we were working to establish a partnership, I would also provide a set of possible new scenarios, in turn allowing the Add-in to better utilize the Office API. In conclusion, I would then present this analysis, my design recommendations along with new feature/scenario ideas to the key contact in the partner company.

The Approach

Scope: A list of Office Apps and networks I used to test the Add-in.

Assumptions: A list of intended uses and interactions.

Areas of Improvement: Elements and interactions we assess the tenets and traps against.

Design Recommendations: Design improvements that utilize the Microsoft design language and pattern library.

Possible New Scenarios: New Add-in features.

Lucidchart

Scope

  • Excel 2016 Desktop Client
  • Word 2016 Desktop Client

Assumptions

  • User has familiarity with Lucidchart services
  • User has premade charts and diagrams
  • Discovery of Add-in happens through Office Store

Areas of Improvement

The Lucidchart analysis began in the Office store. Not only were we providing our best design recommendations, but we also utilized the Microsoft guidelines to assist in naming conventions and image layout.

The Add-in example shows that at the time, the current Add-in lacked a consistent diagram picker, had an easily misunderstood preview placeholder image, and multiple buttons linking to the same location.

Design Recommendations

First Run Experience: The implementation of a first run experience provides the user a better understanding of what the product is and why it's worth their time to use it.

Better Diagram Picker UX: The goal of an improved picker experience is to alleviate confusion when searching for diagrams. Improved empty states, better commanding and more consistency with the online experience were my recommendations.

Thomson Reuters

Scope

  • Outlook 365 Web Application

Assumptions

  • User has familiarity with Thomson Reuters Software
  • User is working with many people from multiple organizations

Areas of Improvement

In this example my focus was on elements that could be misinterpreted and in some cases hard to see. We strongly urged developers to heed accessibility guidelines created by Microsoft regarding text/contrast ratios.

In an effort to provide a quality experience to their users, it is essential that consistency in both design and content be maintained. Consistency is one factor that can separate a haphazard experience from a polished one.

Design Recommendations

Enhanced Persona Cards: Trying to establish a Microsoft look and feel is a major want by many 3rd parties. Accomplishing this gives the user a sense of comfort. I was able to repurpose some of the Microsoft Fabric components and allow the Add-in to achieve the look and feel I was seeking.

Improved Navigation: Helping users navigate should be one of the highest priorities of any website or application. In this example, I wanted to provide a menu structure users were familiar with and one that was easily accessible from anywhere in the Add-in.

Pickit

Scope

  • Microsoft Word 2016
  • Microsoft PowerPoint 2016

Assumptions

  • Users want to be able to save photos for later use
  • Users do not want to pay for photos
  • Using the search bar to find images will be the main navigational feature
  • Users want to easily refine search results

Areas of Improvement

The Office ribbon can sometimes be an area where Add-in developers tend to overlook small details. In the image above, a ceiling and baseline have been marked to indicate the ribbon boundaries. In order to help the partner achieve the necessary look and feel, we provided them with required icon sizes as well as the recommended inner padding.

In this particular instance the focus was on the functionality rather than the User Interface. Considerable attention was placed on the search feature. Based upon our studies we found that users were confident in using a search bar when looking for images. Users lost confidence when images popped up that didn’t fit the search criteria.

Design Recommendations

Improved Image Details: Here I suggested adding photo details that will help users determine file size, image dimensions, file types, etc. Providing details such as file types will help a user determine if their files are too large to send over email or submit to some services. Additionally, providing image dimensions will help determine whether or not a photo becomes pixelated when manipulating the size.

Improved Sorting: In this recommendation I suggested more refinement options such as downloaded most frequently (popularity), highest rated, color, and size. The improved sorting provides the user a greater sense of control allowing them to tailor the image search results to fit color palettes, genres and layouts.

Project 03|Add-in Design Patterns

The Project

The development and design choices with regard to Office Add-ins have historically lacked value and aesthetics. This being the case, the Office store has suffered with both quantity and quality of Add-ins. My team and I were hired to create a set of design patterns utilizing the Microsoft Fabric framework and work with developers to build code samples for 3rd parties, with hopes of changing the negative trajectory.

My Role

I was tasked with designing a set of patterns designers and developers could reference when building key parts of their Add-in. I also utilized my development knowledge and helped build out code samples for a small set of patterns.

Strategy

The beginning of each design followed a very simple strategy, find a few existing Add-ins using the pattern we are developing, assess what worked, what didn’t work, and use our design knowledge to make it better.
